Health Risks Comparisons Across the US

Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Particular Demographics


Healthcare Availability

As may be expected, states with lower percentages of residents lacking healthcare tend to see lower percentages of obesity. For example, Massachusetts has the lowest percentage of residents lacking healthcare, and also has one of the lowest percentages of obesity across the United States. On the other hand, Texas has the highest percentage of residents lacking healthcare and also has one of the highest percentages of obesity in the country.

It can be seen that having access to healthcare does not seem to affect the percentage of residents that smoke. Massachusetts and Texas appear of opposite ends of the spectrum when it comes to residents having heathcare. However, the percentage of residents that smoke is almost the same. It seems most of the country shows residents that smoke to be approximately 18%. Some of the higher smoker percentages come from states with higher percentages of residents lacking healthcare (Between 14-20%). However, the 2 highest smoker percentages come from 2 states with relatively good access to healthcare (West Virginia and Kentucky).

There appears to be a fairly strong correlation across the country that states with higher poverty rates also tend to have greater percentages of residents lacking healthcare. This is an unfortunate reality, as those that live in poverty may not be able to afford healthcare.

Household Income Correlations

There seems to be a downward trend across states when it comes to obesity percentage and median household income. This indicates that, as household income increases, the percentage of obesity decreases.

Interestingly, there also appears to be a downward trend when looking at smoking percentage and median household income. As median household income increases, the smoking percentage decreases. However, this trend appears to flatten around an income of $55,000, showing smoking percentage of approximately 15%. This plateau stays consistent through the higher incomes available in this study.

Finally, as expected, the percetange of residents in poverty appears to be inversely proportional with median household income. Washington DC is an interesting outlier, in that it has a median household income on the higher end of the spectrum, but also a high percentage of residents in poverty.

Age Correlations

When looking at age across the US, there does not seem to be an obvious correlation to obesity percentage.

When comparing states with lower median age to states with higher median age, it can be seen there is also not a significant trend when looking at smoking either. However Utah (with a low median age and low smoking percentage) and West Virginia (with a high median age and high smoking percentage) do appear to skew this particular graph to appear as though states with lower median age also have lower smoking percentages.

Finally, there does not seem to be a particular correlation of age and poverty.

It is interesting to see that Utah's median age is much lower than every other state, making it appear as an outlier in these comparisons.
